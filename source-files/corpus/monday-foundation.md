# monday.com Foundation & Social Impact — From Elevate 2025

## Philosophy
Roy Mann and Eran Zinman see monday.com as more than a successful company. They want to use their abilities as a tech company — not just money, but brainpower and technology — to make real change in the world.

> "We care deeply that we're able to use our abilities as a tech company to give back. Not only putting money aside, but putting our employees' brains into it." — Roy Mann

## Emergency Response Team (ERT)

monday.com has a dedicated emergency response team — essentially a "monday 911."

When disasters happen anywhere in the world, the ERT team physically travels there and deploys monday's technology to help coordinate relief efforts.

### Scale (2025)
- **58 disasters** responded to
- **38 countries** covered
- Deployments range from natural disasters to humanitarian crises

### How It Works
- Team engages immediately when disasters occur
- Travels to the location
- Builds custom monday workflows for aid organizations on the ground
- Coordinates volunteers, aid distribution, community needs mapping
- Leaves organizations equipped to handle future crises

### Case Study: Bahia Blanca, Argentina (March 2025)
- One of the most destructive floods in the city's history
- **350,000+ people** affected
- monday ERT supported **6 aid organizations** on the ground
- Built digital tools for aid distribution, needs mapping, volunteer coordination
- Systems built in days, scaled across the city

> "We didn't just offer a platform. We offered hands-on support to build custom workflows directly with the people needing the response."

## Tech School

A year-long program bringing technology education to underserved communities. Teaching kids that they can expect more, do more, and choose a different future.

### What They Teach
- 3D printing
- AI fundamentals
- Design
- Learn by doing — hands-on, not theoretical

### Impact
- Students visit monday offices, meet employees
- Culminates in makeathons — competitive events where students brainstorm, prototype, and present
- **1,200+ students and teachers** at makeathon events
- Real-world projects: puzzles for blind children (Israel), adaptive tools for kids with disabilities (NYC)

### 2026 Plans
- **150 schools**
- **5,500 students**
- Expanding to New York, London, and Israel

### The Human Impact
> "Nothing excites us more than getting text messages from kids saying 'I didn't know I could do that. You changed my perspective on what I could achieve.'" — Eran Zinman
