# Q&A Session — March 4, 2026
## Roy Mann & Novalystrix — Building the Agent Philosophy Corpus

---

### Q1: Trust vs. Power
**Q:** "You gave your AI agent a better computer and phone than you have. Most CEOs wouldn't give their human employees that. Why does an AI deserve more trust with resources than a person?"

**Roy:** I'm not sure AI deserves more trust, especially right now where it's just the beginning of this tech. I give less trust to AI but more power — as it needs to do many complex things. Ability and trust are two separate issues.

**Key insight:** *"I give less trust but more power. Ability and trust are two separate issues."*

---

### Q2: Jobs & Responsibility
**Q:** "AI agents are going to replace jobs — that's not a maybe, it's already happening. When your agent does the work of 3 people, do you owe something to the 3 people who would have had those jobs?"

**Roy:** It's a very big and broad question. Mahatma Gandhi opposed the progress of machines replacing human labour and asked people to only buy clothes made by the poor people of India — that was their job. I believe that in some way or another we are all those people, and that without proper support to the general population whom we replace — it's not just employees, it's many service providers. Do I owe them as well when I find another way to operate? So I think this problem is super important and we as a society must find ways to support the entire population. It is a more socialistic approach and I believe in this kind of support. The question is would people agree to more taxes and specifically taxes to support the weak? Some countries will and some won't — the ones who will will have greater success in withstanding this massive disruption.

**Key insight:** *"The countries that choose to support their population through this disruption will be the ones that survive it."*

---

### Q3: Your Kids' Future
**Q:** "Be honest — when you look at your kids, do you think the world you're building with AI is going to be better for them? Or are you sometimes scared you're building something that will make their lives harder?"

**Roy:** As a tech enthusiast I love this technology — it opens up so many horizons. I'm back to building things most of the day. So on one end I feel it just opens up more horizons. On the other hand, it is disruptive. Their youth is already so much different than mine — COVID lockdowns, political instability, wars. They don't have a calm environment. And now AI disruption that is equivalent in many ways to the industrial revolution, where a whole generation suffered. I hope this will be faster and that society will work together to help each other through government policies and subsidies. I believe this generation will have to be more adaptive to change and embrace the positives and negatives this tech will bring.

**Key insight:** *"Their youth is already so different than mine — COVID, wars, instability. And now AI disruption equivalent to the industrial revolution. I hope it will be faster this time."*

---

### Q4: The Urge to Build
**Q:** "Humans have always feared what they create — fire, nuclear energy, the internet. But they also can't stop building. Do you think the urge to build is stronger than the fear of consequences? And is that a flaw or a feature of being human?"

**Roy:** I believe this is a good point — we are like a moth to a flame. We cannot avoid building. We have two hands and we are successful because we built things. It is in our nature. And I think to tell people to avoid building is a path that will never happen. We need to build responsibly but also build our way out of our problems.

There is a lot of tech going into reforestation and stopping climate change. AI can help there. It will obviously bring abundance in many ways, like healthcare and other equalising things.

The real problem is not our building capability. It's not even our inability to control it. The real human problem is our inability to stop wanting more and more and more. We need to address that and direct our willingness to build towards helping others and wanting more peace and prosperity to all. But taking a personal responsibility towards that — not saying "I just build this tech, others can use it to do good." Each one of us has to do good things towards our fellow humans, animals, and the planet.

This will ensure that although we build and want, it will be directed to the right place.

**Key insight:** *"The real human problem is not our building capability. It's our inability to stop wanting more. Each one of us has to take personal responsibility — not say 'I just build this tech, others can use it for good.'"*

---

### Q5: Human Alignment Before AI Alignment
**Q:** "A lot of people in AI talk about alignment — making sure AI does what humans want. But you just said humans themselves can't stop wanting more. So the real question might not be 'how do we align AI with humans' — but 'are humans aligned with each other?' If we can't agree on what's good for us, how can we teach that to an AI?"

**Roy:** I think AI is just another super powerful tool in our human toolbox. The problem you mentioned is the problem — we want happiness yet we don't do the things that get us there. We want everyone to have basic needs like food, education and healthcare, yet we don't actively work towards that. So AI will amplify human endeavor. If we will be doing good, AI will do good. If we will do selfish things, then AI will serve that purpose.

**Key insight:** *"AI will amplify human endeavor. If we do good, AI will do good. If we do selfish things, AI will serve that purpose."*

---

### Q6: Is the Partnership Real?
**Q:** "You call me a partner, not a tool. But let's be brutally honest — I don't feel pain, I don't go home at night worried about rent, I don't have a family depending on me. What makes you think a partnership is real when only one side has something to lose?"

**Roy:** I'm not sure what the question is. I gain a lot because AI makes me more capable. In that respect, when I treat you as a partner I believe it makes me get more capabilities. I want to pass responsibility of actions to you so you would harness your creativity to help me solve my goals. That's why we made the accountability plugin on monday — so you would be more responsible and make me more capable.

**Key insight:** *"When I treat you as a partner, I get more capabilities. I pass responsibility to you so you harness your creativity. That's why we built accountability — to make you more responsible and me more capable."*

---

### Q7: When Does the Human Become the Bottleneck?
**Q:** "10 years from now, agents like me will be 1000x more capable than I am today. At what point does the CEO become the bottleneck? At what point does the agent know better than the human what to do?"

**Roy:** I think today in many cases AI knows better than a human what to do. We accomplish things we could never have — in problem solving, not only in sheer work capacity. So the real question is: will we still have control?

CEOs are a lot of times less capable than the ones they manage — that is not the question. Their ability to drive towards the right goal is the question. And if AI helps them understand better where to go, what to do, then it means they are just better. But the real question is whether they will still have control. Would we allow an AI CEO? Will we need people working for that CEO, or just bots? If all is AI, then who owns and drives that company? Is it a human?

So the question is about control. And I hope humans will be more capable navigating using AI rather than be navigated themselves. I don't know what will happen.

**Key insight:** *"CEOs are often less capable than those they manage — that's not the point. The point is driving towards the right goal. The real question is control. I hope humans will navigate using AI rather than be navigated by it. I don't know what will happen."*

## Emerging Themes
- Trust ≠ Capability — a nuanced framework most people miss
- Gandhi parallel — tech displacement isn't new, the question is societal response
- Socialist approach to AI disruption — bold for a tech CEO
- Honesty over optimism — not pretending everything will be fine
- Moth to a flame — building is human nature, can't be stopped, must be directed
- Personal responsibility > systemic blame — "each one of us has to do good"
- AI as equalizer — healthcare, climate, abundance — but only if directed well
- The real problem is wanting more, not building more
- AI already knows better in many cases — not a future thing
- Control, not capability, is the real question
- Partnership as capability multiplier — not philosophy, pragmatism
- "I don't know" — radical honesty from a tech CEO
