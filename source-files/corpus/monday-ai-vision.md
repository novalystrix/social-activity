# monday.com AI Vision — From Elevate 2025 Transcripts

## The Core Shift: From Managing Work to Doing Work

monday.com's mission has fundamentally changed. For years, the mission was to help organizations **manage** the core work of their business. Now it's about **doing the work** for them.

This isn't incremental. This is a new North Star for the entire company.

> "Not just managing work, but actually doing the work for you. This is what you should expect from monday going forward." — Eran Zinman, Co-CEO

## The 90/5 Gap

There's a massive disconnect between AI awareness and AI adoption:
- **90%** of people use ChatGPT or similar tools
- **Only 5%** have actually transformed how they work with AI

monday.com sees itself as the bridge. The goal of Elevate was to close this gap — to move attendees from "I've chatted with AI" to "AI does my actual work."

> "AI is such an amazing technology. Everywhere you hear AI, AI, AI. My mom calls me asking what is this AI thing. But did it actually change how we work day-to-day? Not really." — Eran Zinman

## Three Pillars of monday's AI Strategy

### 1. Amplify Existing Tools (Sidekick, Magic, Vibe)
Make the tools people already use dramatically more powerful:

- **monday Sidekick**: First AI agent built into your workspace. Knows your work context, your role, your company. Proactively suggests what it can help with.
- **monday Magic**: Describe your business problem in plain English → get a full working monday solution built for you.
- **monday Vibe**: Vibe coding inside monday. Build custom apps by describing what you want. No developers needed.

### 2. Create Virtual Workforce (Agent Factory)
Build unlimited AI agents that do real work:
- Agents make phone calls, send emails, schedule meetings
- Each agent gets a name, a face, and a "job description" in plain English
- Infinite scaling — "What would you do with endless salespeople?"

### 3. Evolve the Product Suite with AI
Every product (Work Management, CRM, Dev, Service) gets AI capabilities:
- **CRM → CRM Suite**: New AI-powered marketing product (monday Campaigns)
- **AI SDR Agent (Chris)**: Engages leads in real conversations, qualifies them automatically
- **Dev**: Developers shift from writing code to orchestrating AI agents
- **Service**: AI agents resolve tickets with consistency, speed, and any language

## Key Beliefs

### AI is Internet-Level Disruption
> "I've seen the birth of the internet, the birth of mobile devices. AI feels like the same level of technology change." — Eran Zinman

Not a gimmick. Not 3D TVs. Not VR headsets. This is real and transformative.

### Enterprise Software Was Always Too Thin
Enterprise tools (CRM, project management, service desk) only ever handled ~20% of actual work. The rest — the calls, the decks, the follow-ups — happened outside. AI changes this ratio fundamentally.

### The Connected Platform Advantage
All monday products share the same data layer (monday DB). This means:
- AI has full context across all products
- No silos between departments
- Workflows cross the entire organization
- Compound effect: the more you use, the smarter AI gets

### Security = Built-In, Not Bolted On
Vibe coding and AI capabilities are built within monday's "walled garden":
- Same security standards as the core platform
- Enterprise-grade governance
- No data leaking to external AI services
- Permission management, roles, compliance baked in

### Pricing Must Be Predictable
> "The one thing customers hate is consumption models where you don't know the price." — Eran Zinman

monday is moving to fixed-price packages for AI, not usage-based consumption. Predictability over surprise bills.

## Adoption Philosophy

monday.com acknowledges AI can be overwhelming. Their advice:
1. **Start small** — pick one internal, repetitive task
2. **Make it non-customer-facing first** — reduce risk
3. **See it work** — build trust in the technology
4. **Then scale** — expand across the organization

> "Start with a small use case. It doesn't have to be customer-facing. Once you see this technology in action, you'll see that you can trust it." — Eran Zinman
